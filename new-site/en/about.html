About

Technology Designed for the Field

Our Philosophy

Technology Must Serve the Field

Our founding team is made up of engineers with years of hands-on field experience.

We have always believed:

What truly drives society forward is the work that creates lasting value in the real field.

Whether it is communications, networking, infrastructure, system implementation, or field operations support, these roles — though less comfortable than an office environment — are directly essential to the stable operation and continued development of social systems.

We respect the field, and we understand it.

Because the field needs more than just technology — it needs:

  • Experience
  • Judgment
  • Coordination
  • Responsibility
  • A mindset for long-term stable operations

We believe:

Truly valuable technology must be able to run stably in the real field over the long term.

Technology should do more than just demonstrate features — it should:

  • Reduce risk
  • Increase reliability
  • Support continuous operations
  • Help the field work more efficiently together

Izumo Soan is neither a pure software company nor an ordinary construction firm.

Our goal is to become:

A field problem-solving engineering company

Connecting field, technology, safety, and operations.

Through engineering, technology, and accountability, we create lasting value for social infrastructure and field operations.

The Meaning of Our Name

Connection, Protection, Stability

Izumo

Izumo is one of Japan's most legendary mythological lands. Izumo symbolizes:

  • Connection — where gods gather, where people and technology meet
  • Protection — safety, security, and peace
  • Stability — a stable foundation for continued operations

Soan

"Soan" means creating a safe and reliable operating environment:

  • Network stability
  • System reliability
  • Operational continuity
  • Field safety
  • Information transparency

Problems We Aim to Solve

We focus on the four most common challenges in field operations

Disconnect Between Technology and the Field

Even the best technology is meaningless if it does not match field conditions and operational needs.

Short-Term Perspective

Many solutions work well at deployment but fail to consider long-term maintenance and scalability.

Information Silos

Different systems cannot share information, making it impossible to see the full picture needed for decision-making.

Inability to Handle Complexity

System integration in multi-vendor environments and adaptation to Japan's unique construction practices pose real difficulties.

Our Engineering Philosophy

Four principles guiding our technical practice

"The essence of software is managing state changes in the real world"

What matters is not the code itself, but the change that code brings about in the field.

"Real value comes from solving field problems"

Meeting technical specifications is not enough. Value lies in solving actual field problems.

Engineers Must Speak the Language of the Field

It is not enough to know technical jargon — engineers must understand the needs and constraints of field personnel.

Pursuing Simplicity and Reliability

Rather than over-engineering, we provide solutions that the field truly needs and can use over the long term.
